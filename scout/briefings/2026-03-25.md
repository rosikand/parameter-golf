### Technique Combos: Helpful vs. Harmful

**Strong combos:**
- `INT6 + Zstd-22 + QAT + magnitude pruning` — the space-efficient backbone enabling 11L at <16MB
- `XSA (last 4L) + BigramHash + SmearGate` — complementary: XSA handles long range, BigramHash local context, SmearGate smooth gating
- `Hedge Mixer + TTT` — online n-gram interpolation during TTT eval is additive (-0.07 BPB TTT gain in #688)
- `CROWN-Q + INT6/INT5` — reduces quantization error AND artifact size; novel combo with measurable gain
- `stride=64 eval + extra TTT epoch` — exploiting budget tradeoff: faster eval → more TTT budget

**Harmful or null combos (from issue #140):**
- `Sequence curriculum + SWA` — curriculum breaks SWA; don't combine
- `EMA + SWA on quantized models` — EMA causes 0.14 BPB quant gap on SWA stacks; use one or the other
- `Multi-epoch TTT (any form)` — **closed for every attempt**; the competition is extremely strict here

### Emerging Techniques Worth Watching

1. **CROWN-Q** (PR #700): Quantization-aware loss penalty `λ·mean(w²·Δ²/12)` during warmdown. Saves ~200KB AND improves quant-time accuracy. Only 1 submission — highly underexplored.
2. **K-Projection LoRA TTT** (PR #611): Adapting K projections at 0.3× LR. Disqualified due to Min-NLL, but the K-proj adaptation concept itself is legal and untested in a compliant TTT framework.
3. **5-gram/n-gram eval interpolation** (PR #674): Legal cache-from-scored-tokens-only n-gram mix. Strong directional signal; closed only for submission hygiene issues, not the concept itself.
4. **Int5 quantization** (PR #700): Enables ~200KB extra model capacity vs Int6. Underexplored — only 1 submission.
5. **FlashAttention-3** (PR #605, #614): ~3% wall-clock speedup. Available for legal use as pure efficiency gain.
6. **Depth-scaled residuals 1/√(layer+1)** (PR #512): avg BPB 0.868 across 2 submissions — tiny sample but very strong signal.
7. **Codebook+Huffman compression** (PR #532): 21% smaller artifact vs INT6+zstd, enabling larger model. Author self-closed for TTT violation; the compression pipeline itself is legal.

## Frontier Gaps & Opportunities

Ranked by estimated BPB impact, highest first:

### 1. ✅ Legal Single-Epoch LoRA TTT on Strong Architecture (~−0.10 to −0.15 BPB expected)
**Status:** The compliant LoRA TTT baseline is around 1.07–1.09. The disqualified submissions (PRs #568, #596, #611) show 0.78–0.95 BPB is achievable — the gap is almost entirely due to multi-epoch illegal scoring.  
**What to try:** Single-pass backward-looking LoRA TTT with: (a) rank-8 Q/V + rank-16 LM-head, (b) per-layer LR groups (V: 1.5×, Q: 0.5×, K: 0.3×, LM-head: 2×, bias: 3×), (c) cosine LR within the single epoch using chunk-level mini-steps. This is legal because you score each chunk before training on it — cosine decay within-epoch is fine.  
**Untried combo:** K-proj LoRA (from #611) + legal single-epoch framework. K-proj at 0.3× LR has never been tested under compliant scoring.

### 2. ✅ CROWN-Q + INT5 + Larger Model (~−0.03 to −0.05 BPB)
**Status:** PR #700 introduced both CROWN-Q and INT5 but didn't push model size to the new limit they enable.  
**What to try:** With Int5+zstd-22 saving ~200KB vs Int6, you can fit a wider model (e.g., 576d or extra attention heads) within 16MB. Pair with CROWN-Q penalty λ∈[0.001, 0.01] tuned during warmdown. Neither the size expansion nor the λ sweep has been done.

### 3. ✅ 5-gram Eval Cache + Hedge Mixer (Legal, ~−0.02 to −0.04 BPB over #700)
**Status:** PR #674 showed 5-gram interpolation at α=0.20 is directionally legal. PR #700 uses 5-expert Hedge but without the n-gram eval cache from #674.  
**What to try:** Combine #700's Hedge mixer with #674's score-first 5-gram cache (built strictly from already-scored tokens). The Hedge weights would then adapt over 6 experts: neural + unigram + bigram + trigram + 5-gram + entropy. **This combo has never been attempted.**

### 4. ✅ Codebook+Huffman Compression + Legal TTT (~−0.02 to −0.04 BPB)
**Status:** PR #532 showed 14.12MB vs 18+MB with INT6+zstd — a 21% reduction, enabling a meaningfully larger model. Author self-closed only for TTT violation; compression is legal.  
**What to try:** Rebuild PR #532's CB-48/CB-80/CB-64 codebook+Huffman pipeline on top of #700's architecture, use freed space for wider MLP or more layers, add legal single-epoch LoRA TTT. **Codebook compression + TTT has never been combined in a compliant submission.**

### 5. ✅ Depth-Scaled Residuals + Current Best Stack (~−0.02 BPB)
**Status:** Only 2 submissions use depth-scaled residuals `1/√(layer+1)`, with avg BPB 0.868. This is the strongest technique-by-avg-BPB in the dataset after excluding disqualified entries.  
**What to try:** Add `1/√(layer+1)` residual scaling to the #700 base architecture. Costs zero parameters. **Has never been combined with Hedge Mixer, CROWN-Q, or Int5.**

### 6. ✅ FlashAttention-3 on Legal Submission (~−0.01 BPB via extra TTT budget)
**Status:** FA3 gives ~3% wall-clock speedup (PRs #605, #614). At 10-minute budget, 3% ≈ 18 extra seconds — enough for more TTT steps.  
**What to try:** Port FA3 from #605 into #700's codebase. The rotary cache clone fix is documented in #605. Pure efficiency gain, no compliance risk.
