### Technique Combos That Help

1. **EMA + Tight SWA stacking** (PR #414, #401): These two averaging signals are orthogonal. EMA tracks a smooth running average throughout training; Tight SWA collects late-stage checkpoints from the EMA-averaged weights. Combined: ~–0.001 BPB over either alone.

2. **GPTQ-lite + Late QAT**: GPTQ-lite operates post-training (zero cost), QAT operates during warmdown. Both address quantization error from different angles and stack cleanly (PR #414 documents both contributing ~–0.0006 and ~–0.0001 respectively).

3. **AdamW TTT + score-first protocol**: PR #442 shows AdamW TTT is 40% faster than SGD TTT while better. PR #473 and #503 show score-first chunking (evaluate chunk then adapt on it) is the legally sound implementation.

4. **Value Residual + Gated Attention** (PR #490): Novel combo not yet in multi-seed confirmation. Single-seed improvement of ~0.009 BPB over PR #442's best seed is promising.

### Technique Combos That Hurt or Show Diminishing Returns

- **TrigramHash overhead** (PR #486): TrigramHash + GradQuant added significant compute overhead, resulting in 22% fewer training steps. Net gain over baseline was only –0.023 BPB; unclear if the techniques are worth the step budget cost without further optimization.
- **XSA on all 11 layers** (PR #503 vs #417): Extending XSA to all layers vs. last-4 gave marginal improvement (1.1218 vs 1.1227 mean) at higher compute cost. Not a clear win.
- **LoRA TTT with high epoch count** (PR #512): Multi-epoch LoRA TTT introduces extreme seed variance (std ≈ 0.068). This instability likely reflects TTT compliance issues rather than genuine model behavior.

### Emerging Techniques Worth Watching

1. **GradQuant / adaptive quantization** (PR #486): Differentiating quantization precision by gradient sensitivity (Int5/6/7 per tensor) is novel. Currently adds overhead but could be optimized. If the overhead is resolved, adaptive quant could replace fixed Int6 across the board.
2. **Value Residual** (PR #490, #486): Caching V from layer 0 and blending into later layers adds only ~2 params/layer and appears to give ~–0.005 BPB. Highly parameter-efficient; not yet in any 3-seed confirmed record.
3. **Gated Attention** (PR #490): Per-head sigmoid gate with init bias=4.0 adds 4104 params/layer and appears complementary to Value Residual. Avg BPB for "Gated Attention" is artificially high (1.92) due to few submissions, but the mechanism is sound.
4. **Parameter Banking + Parallel Muon** (PR #473, #399): Achieves 81.87ms/step, gaining ~200–300 extra training steps vs. standard Muon. Only benefits EMA-based (not SWA/TTT) submissions per PR #399 analysis.
