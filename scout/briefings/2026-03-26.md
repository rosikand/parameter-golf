## Technique Analysis

### Dominant Stack (Ranks 2–5, confirmed working)
The top legitimate cluster all share this core:

```
Order-9 to 15-gram backoff
  + Per-order alpha multipliers (orders 2-3: 0.3×, orders 5+: 2.0×)
  + Entropy-adaptive blending (sigmoid formula, per-order centers)
  + Shared/distributed N-gram tables (full 62M-token coverage)
  + Score-first enforcement
  ± Score-first LoRA TTT rank-8 (adds ~0.015 BPB)
  ± Complementary training COMP_ALPHA=0.50 (adds synergistic gain)
  + GPTQ Int5/Int6 quantization
```

BPB trajectory of this stack: neural-only ~1.14 → +N-gram → ~0.63 → +per-order multipliers → ~0.55 → +distributed tables → ~0.50 → +complementary training → ~0.44 → +TTT → ~0.295. Each component is additive and well-ablated across multiple independent PRs.

### Technique Combos That Help

1. **Complementary training × N-gram backoff**: The synergy is multiplicative, not additive. CT makes the neural model specialize on N-gram-hard tokens, allowing much higher eval alpha (20–75% vs 5–60%), which dramatically amplifies the N-gram cache contribution. This is the single most important combo insight this week.

2. **Distributed N-gram prefill × high-order N-grams**: Without full 62M-token cache coverage, high-order N-grams (8-15) see too few hits to be useful. Distributed prefill/shared tables unlock their value.

3. **Per-order multipliers × entropy-adaptive alpha**: Per-order multipliers tune the *relative* trust per order; entropy-adaptive alpha tunes the *absolute* blending strength. Combined they give a 2D control surface that outperforms either alone.

4. **GPTQ Int5 × LoRA TTT**: Quantization frees parameter budget for more capacity; TTT then recovers quant loss at eval time. Net gain positive.

### Technique Combos That Appear Neutral/Negative
- **SWA + EMA + seq curriculum**: Issue #140 notes seq curriculum breaks SWA/EMA, causing a 0.14 BPB quantization gap. Avoid this combination until fixed.
- **Min-NLL TTT**: Universally closed as invalid. Do not attempt.
- **Multi-epoch TTT (score-every-epoch)**: Also closed as invalid.

### Emerging Techniques Worth Watching

1. **Learned routing head (#834)**: If validated, this is the breakthrough of the competition. End-to-end learned gating between neural and N-gram orders eliminates brittle entropy heuristics. The frozen oracle training trick is particularly elegant (decouples table build cost from gradient flow).

2. **EBLS (#796)**: Shared-block LoRA architecture is an efficiency play that keeps the model under 16MB while delivering 11 effective layers. Worth stacking with other techniques.

3. **3D Cubric (#814)**: Adding count-bin as a third dimension to alpha multipliers is a low-implementation-cost upgrade from 2D Cubric that may provide consistent small gains.

4. **GELU pre-enrichment (#810)**: 512→768→512 block before the transformer. Effect is unquantified in isolation but present in a 0.2995 BPB submission — worth ablating.

5. **U-Net Skips (#826)**: Present in the 0.2951 submission. Standard technique but rarely combined with N-gram stacks; may provide non-trivial capacity gains.

## Frontier Gaps & Opportunities

Ranked by estimated expected BPB impact, highest first:

### 1. ✅ Validate & Extend Learned Routing Head (#834) — Expected: −0.05 to −0.10 BPB
**What's untried:** #834's learned routing head is trained on next-token prediction but uses a fixed architecture. Extensions:
- **Deeper routing MLP** (Linear(512→256→7) with ReLU): May capture more complex routing signals.
- **Per-position routing** (routing conditioned on position embedding): High-order N-grams are more reliable in long contexts; position-aware routing could exploit this.
- **Combine learned routing with complementary training**: CT specializes the neural backbone; if the routing head is also trained under CT's reweighted loss, it should learn to route differently on N-gram-hard tokens. No one has tried this combo.

### 2. Complementary Training × Learned Routing Head — Expected: −0.03 to −0.05 BPB
#834 uses backoff TTT but not complementary training. #796/#803 use complementary training but not a learned routing head. Combining them should be additive: CT produces a neural model that handles surprising tokens better → the routing head learns sharper boundaries → higher average confidence in whichever predictor is used. **This specific combo appears in zero current submissions.**

### 3. Higher N-gram Orders (15-20) with Full Coverage — Expected: −0.01 to −0.03 BPB
#796 pushes to 15-grams. No submission has tried orders 16-20 with full distributed prefill. Diminishing returns expected but the jump from 9→15 was measurable (~0.005 BPB per order at that range). Memory cost is the constraint: 8×4M buckets at order-15 requires careful hash sizing.

### 4. 3D Cubric × Complementary Training — Expected: −0.01 to −0.02 BPB
#814 has 3D Cubric + CT but without TTT or Int5. #826/#809 have TTT + Int5 but without Cubric or CT. The full stack (3D Cubric + CT + Int5 + Score-first TTT) exists in no single submission. The Cubric count-bin dimension is particularly relevant under CT because the count distribution of N-gram hits changes when the neural model is trained to specialize on hard tokens.

### 5. EBLS × Complementary Training × Learned Routing — Expected: −0.02 to −0.04 BPB
#796 (EBLS + CT) is at 0.2880. Adding a learned routing head (replacing hand-crafted entropy gating with an end-to-end trained gate) on top of EBLS should work since EBLS just changes the architecture's weight sharing, not the eval-time mixing logic.

### 6. Score-First TTT on Learned Routing Head — Expected: −0.005 to −0.015 BPB
#834 uses score-first TTT on the neural causal mixer. Extending TTT adaptation to also fine-tune the routing head weights (LoRA on the Linear(512→7)) during eval could allow the gate to adapt to domain shifts within the validation set.

### 7. GELU Pre-Enrichment Ablation — Expected: Unknown, potentially −0.005 to −0.02 BPB
#810 includes a GELU pre-enrichment block (512→768→512 before transformer) at 0.2995 BPB, but it has never been ablated in isolation. A clean PR adding only this block to the standard stack would establish its true value. If it contributes even 0.01 BPB, it deserves to be in every top submission.
